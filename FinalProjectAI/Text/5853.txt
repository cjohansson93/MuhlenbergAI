Cross Country Runners Compete at Nationals


Three Muhlenberg cross country runners competed at the NCAA Division III Championships.

Senior Sara Gearin placed 206th in the women’s race, and juniors David Chorney (195th place) and 
Timothy Van Syckle (230th place) represented the men’s team. 
 
One of Muhlenberg’s Centennial Conference rivals, Haverford, finished second in the men’s race.

Two teams wearing purple, the Scranton women and Curry men, made life blue for the Muhlenberg basketball teams, on championship night at the Scotty Wood Tournament.

Both visiting teams grabbed early leads and were never 
teams, Muhlenberg College’s Scotty Wood event is drawing good crowds,

seriously challenged in capturing the tournament titles. Scranton won the women’s title, 84-60, with Curry capturing the men’s crown, 92-71.

In the women’s game, 14th-ranked Scranton went on a 13-0 run in the first five minutes to take command. The Mules showed some life at the start of the second half, scoring the first seven points to cut a 17-point deficit to 10, but the Lady Royals responded with a 12-2 run and breezed the rest of the way.

Sophomore Alexis Bates established a career high in scoring for the second night in a row, netting 16 points, and 
Lauren Boyle added 15. Both were named to the all-tournament team.

Curry made 14 of 21 three-point attempts in the men’s final. The Mules were down only five, 38-33, in the last minute of the first half, but the Colonels scored the last four points of the half and led by double digits for most of the second half.

Sophomore Obi Nwizugbo scored a career-high 13 points on 5-of-7 shooting, and classmate 
Brian Frankoski poured in a career-high 16 points. Nwizugbo and sophomore 
Ryan Foster earned all-tournament honors.
