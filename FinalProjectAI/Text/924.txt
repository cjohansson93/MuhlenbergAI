Course Offering Overview





100-199: Themes and Motifs in the Study of ReligionThese are 100-level courses that introduce students to the academic study of religion by tracing patterns and themes across religious traditions. 
Course descriptions >



 

201/202: Theory & Method in the Study of ReligionOffered annually in the fall, this course is required of all religion studies majors and strongly recommended for minors. Students will explore a variety of approaches to the academic study of religion.
Course descriptions >



 

203-299: Religions of the WorldThese courses are religion-specific, concentrating on a particular religious tradition or clusters of religions.
Course descriptions > 



 

300-349: Religious Expressions (Texts, Rituals/Practices, Fine and Performing Arts)These courses provide opportunities for students to explore diverse forms of religious expression. Each course offers an in-depth look at a phenomenon from a particular religious tradition.
Course descriptions > 



 

350-399: Religion, Person, and SocietyThis is a series of thematic courses that examine specific theoretical, historical, geographical, political, and philosophical contexts for specific religious beliefs and practices. Courses may focus on a particular religious tradition or may emphasize theoretical approaches that apply across traditions.
Course descriptions >
