Men’s Golf Flies Second at Messiah



Rough Night for Other Teams

Men’s Soccer

Muhlenberg had its four-game winning streak snapped with a 1-0 loss at Dickinson on an unassisted first-half goal. The Mules (6-3, 2-1) entered the match ranked sixth in the Middle Atlantic Region, while the Red Devils (7-3, 2-1), who were ranked in the top 25 last week, were seventh.

Volleyball

Two-time defending Centennial Conference champion Haverford turned away upset-minded Muhlenberg for a 3-1 win.

The Mules (11-6, 1-2) won the second set, 25-18, and were tied late in the first (20-20) and fourth sets (16-16), but the Fords, who are ranked 24th in Division III, secured their seventh straight series win.

Junior Phylicia Lee led Muhlenberg with 18 kills, and junior Brittany Williams had a career-high 24 digs. Freshman Davi Gordon recorded two aces and a kill early in the second set, helping the Mules open up a commanding 10-1 lead.

Field Hockey

The Mules had their two-game winning streak snapped with a 3-1 home loss to Haverford.

Junior Tara Cohn scored her fifth goal in three games with 4:52 on the clock, cutting a 2-0 deficit in half, but the Fords came right back and scored 23 seconds later.

The Muhlenberg men’s golf team finished second for the second straight tournament, taking the runner-up spot at the Messiah Falcon Classic.

The Mules shot a 307, five strokes behind Rutgers-Camden. They won the tournament last year with a 301.

Sophomore Zachary Oyer posted the low Muhlenberg score for the third time in as many competitions this fall. His 3-over-par 75 tied for fourth with four other golfers, including senior Bill Snow. Oyer (right) leads the Mules with a 74. 8 stroke average.

Freshman Matthew Cohen also landed in the top 10 in his collegiate debut, carding a 77.
