men’s basketball falls just short





Liddic, fouled on this play, scored 11 of his 15 points in the second half.


"The slimmest of margins" just got even slimmer.

The Muhlenberg 



men's basketball team suffered a heartbreaking 67-66 loss at Ursinus when Evan Tozer's jumper from the corner with 1.3 seconds left was ruled a two-pointer.

The Mules (8-9, 4-5), who rallied from a 13-point halftime deficit behind the shooting of freshman Austin Curry, trailed 67-64 when they took possession after a pair of Bear free throws with 20.9 seconds left. A three-pointer from the wing missed, but the rebound got batted out to Tozer in the corner. With time running down, the senior leaned into his defender in an attempt to draw a foul as he heaved the ball toward the basket. The ball went in, but in moving forward Tozer stepped over the three-point line.

Ursinus (6-10, 3-6) inbounded the ball and ran out the clock.
