FIELD HOCKEY PICKS UP ROAD SHUTOUT






WOMEN’S TENNIS GETS VICTORY UNDER THE LIGHTS


The Muhlenberg women’s tennis team opened up its portion of the fall
season with
a
5-4 win over Gwynedd-Mercy in the first night home match in the history of
the program.

Sophomore Carly Rotfeld, playing No. 2, led the way for the Mules,
winning
both her doubles and singles matches without losing a game. She teamed up
with freshman Danielle Weiss for the doubles win.

Sophomore Jenn Powell (No. 1) and senior Pam Kimmelman (No.
3) notched the
other victories for &#146Berg, teaming up for the doubles win, 8-2, and
each
winning their singles matches in straight sets.

Playing the majority of the game on its offensive side of the field,
Muhlenberg (5-2) peppered the Rangers with 28 shots and finally reached
the scoring column just before halftime.
<P>
Forward <b>Jillian Duffy</b> notched her fifth goal of the season eight
minutes
before intermission. Though they got off a season-high 18 first-half
shots, the home &#146keeper made 10 saves to keep the score close.
<P>
Senior forward <b>LisaAnn Polt</b> made the score 2-0 with her fourth goal
on the
year, giving Muhlenberg a little breathing room, before classmate
<b>Jessica
Hand</b> found the back of the cage with a rebound in the 67th minute for
her
11th career goal.
<p>
