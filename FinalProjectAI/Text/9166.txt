The President's Committee on Sustainability
The President's Committee on Sustainability aims to provide leadership to the Muhlenberg community in support of the Collegeâs strategic goals of sustainability and good environmental citizenship.
The Committee was created in 2003 by President Helm to examine, propose and monitor sustainability efforts, reduce resource use and promote dialogue on- and off-campus. President Williams reinforced the Committee's charge in 2015. The Committee is composed of faculty, staff and student representatives. Current members include:

Kent Dyer, Chief Business Officer & Treasurer, Co-Chair
Richard Niesenbaum, Director of Sustainability Studies and Professor of Biology, Co-Chair
Morgan Tietz, Sustainability Specialist
Kammie Takahashi, Faculty, Religion Studies
