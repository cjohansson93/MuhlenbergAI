Turnovers costly in football loss


The Muhlenberg football team committed four second-half turnovers and let a 10-0 lead slip away in 



Dandridge was the first Mule freshman to rush for 100 yards in the first game of his career since 1977.



dropping its season opener to Wilkes, 14-12.

The Mules scored on their 

first possession of the game, driving 63 yards in eight plays and netting three points on a 17-yard field goal by junior Michael Katz. Early in the 



Junior linebacker Andy Curley ran 30 yards on a fake punt late in the third quarter and assisted on four tackles, including a sack.



second quarter, freshman Terrence Dandridge rushed 5 yards to cap an 83-yard, nine-play drive and put the Mules up 10-0.

The Colonels scored their first touchdown with a 37-yard run on fourth-and-1 with five minutes left in the third quarter. Muhlenberg drove to the Wilkes 5 on the ensuing possession, but on the first play of the fourth quarter an interception in the end zone foiled the scoring attempt.

Midway through the fourth, Wilkes picked off a pass and returned it 28 yards to the Mule 15. On the next play, the Colonels threw the go-ahead touchdown pass.

Muhlenberg drove into Wilkes territory again on its next possession, only to throw another interception with 1:56 left 

The Colonels took an intentional safety with 5 seconds left, and the Mules fumbled the final kickoff.

Dandridge rushed for 120 yards on 29 carries in his first career game. Freshman Justin Kreamer threw for 212 yards on 17-of-30 passing but was intercepted three times.

The Mules outgained the Colonels, 381-261, and had a 10:30 advantage in time of possession. Junior Scott Gavin led the defense with seven tackles.
