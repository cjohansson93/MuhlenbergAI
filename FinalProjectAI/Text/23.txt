 Major Requirements -- Russian Studies Program Page

Students majoring in Russian Studies must successfully complete RUS 204 - Intermediate Russian II with a grade point average of at least 2.00 or demonstrate an equivalent command of the language. All students must maintain a grade point average of at least 2.00 in all courses completed for the major and minor in Russian Studies. 
Majors take nine courses beyond RUS 102 - Elementary Russian II, including:
Language:RUS 203: Intermediate Russian IRUS 204: Intermediate Russian IIRUS 301: Russian Conversation & CompositionRUS 304: Advanced Russian Conversation & CompositionRUS 380: Business Russian
Literature:RUS 305: Readings in Russian LiteratureRUS 402: Twentieth Century Russian Literature in Translation
Culture: RUS 320: Russian Culture and Civilization 
Politics:PSC 234: Russian Government and Politics
Electives: (Number required is dependent on Russian language proficiency)HST 307, 308: Orthodox Christianity: A Root of RussiaHST 250: Europe: From Cold War to Unification, 1945 to present PSC 201: Political IdeologiesECN 334: International Trade and Globalization (if research project is focused on Russia)BUS 341: Globalization and Marketing (if Export Plan is focused on Russia)
CUE:RUS 490: Russia and the Near Abroad Minor Requirements

Students minoring in Russian Studies take six courses beyond RUS 102 - Elementary Russian II, including:
Literature:  RUS 402: 20th Century Russian Literature in Translation
Culture: RUS 320: Russian Culture and Civilization
Electives: (Number required is dependent on Russian language proficiency)RUS 203: Intermediate Russian I RUS 204: Intermediate Russian II RUS 301: Russian Conversation & CompositionRUS 304: Advanced Russian Conversation & Composition RUS 305: Readings in Russian LiteratureRUS 380: Business RussianHST 307: Orthodox Christianity: A Root of RussiaPSC 234, 235: Government & Politics of Russia 
Course Offerings

Language:RUS 101 & 102: Elementary Russian I & II (1 course unit each)RUS 203 & 204: Intermediate Russian I & II (1 course unit each)
Culture, Literature and Cinema (offered in Russian):RUS 301, 302: (W) Russian Conversation & CompositionRUS 304: (W) Advanced Russian Conversation and Composition RUS 305, 306: (W) Readings in Russian LiteratureRUS 380: Business RussianCUE: Russia and the Near Abroad (.5 credit)
Culture, Literature, Politics, History and Religion (offered in English):RUS 320: Russian Culture & Civilization (DE)RUS 402: Twentieth Century Russian Literature in Translation (DE)PSC 234, 235 (W): Government & Politics of RussiaHST 307, 308 (W): Orthodox Christianity: A Root of Russia HST 249, 250 (W): From Cold War to Unification PSC 201: Political IdeologiesECN 334: International Trade and Globalization BUS 341: Globalization and Marketing
